The recommended tire pressure can usually be found in your vehicle's owner's manual or on a sticker located on the driver's side door jamb or inside the fuel filler door. It is important to follow the manufacturer's recommended tire pressure for optimal performance, safety, and fuel efficiency. Typically, the recommended pressure ranges between 30 to 35 PSI (pounds per square inch) for most passenger cars.

The check engine light can illuminate for various reasons, ranging from minor issues to more significant problems. It is an indicator that something is not functioning properly in your vehicle's engine or emissions system. Common causes include a loose gas cap, faulty oxygen sensor, catalytic converter issues, or engine misfires. It is recommended to have a mechanic use diagnostic tools to identify the specific cause and address the underlying problem.


The lifespan of a car battery can vary depending on factors such as climate, driving conditions, and battery quality. On average, most car batteries last between 3 to 5 years. However, it is a good practice to have your battery tested during routine maintenance appointments to ensure it is functioning optimally. If you experience signs of a weak battery, such as slow engine cranking or frequent need for jump-starts, it may be time to replace it.


A grinding noise when braking is usually an indication of worn brake pads or damaged brake rotors. When the brake pads wear down, the metal backing plate can come into contact with the rotor, causing the grinding sound. It is important to address this issue promptly, as driving with worn brake components can compromise your vehicle's braking efficiency and safety. Have a professional mechanic inspect and replace the necessary brake components.
